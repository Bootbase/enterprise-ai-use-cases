## Source Quality Notes

The evidence base for this use case is strong, anchored by three independent Forrester Total Economic Impact studies (S1, S2, S10) that provide quantified ROI based on multi-customer interviews with defined composite organizations. These are the most credible sources for cost and benefit claims. Platform-scale metrics from Avalara (S4) and Vertex (S5) are vendor-published but verifiable — Vertex is publicly traded and reports financials to the SEC; Avalara's transaction volumes are corroborated by its public financial history. The Sovos State of Tax Compliance Report (S6) is a vendor-published industry survey — useful for market context and regulatory statistics but not independent research. Thomson Reuters product launch claims (S3) regarding 40–60% preparation time reduction and 75% audit exposure reduction are marketing figures, not independently verified, though directionally consistent with the Forrester findings. The EU ViDA regulation (S7) is authoritative regulatory documentation from the European Commission. The Vertex AI announcement (S8) and EY agentic platform press release (S9) are vendor announcements describing capabilities and deployment scale rather than measured customer outcomes. The Accenture $3.2M deduction claim referenced in the research brief originates from a secondary aggregation source and could not be traced to an Accenture primary publication — it is not used as a primary claim in this case study.
